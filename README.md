# Results & Conclusions

- The overall employee attrition rate indicates opportunities for improvement in retention strategies.
- Departments such as Sales and Human Resources stand out with higher attrition rates.
- Job roles like "Sales Representative" and "Laboratory Technician" experience pronounced turnover, warranting further role-specific investigation.
- A clear trend emerges showing employees with lower incomes are more likely to leave, underscoring the importance of competitive compensation.
- Attrition is concentrated in employees with shorter tenures, emphasizing the need to improve support during early employment stages.

---

# Recommendations for HR to Address Employee Attrition

1. **Department-Specific Retention Initiatives:**  
   Develop targeted engagement and support initiatives in departments with high attrition rates.

2. **Compensation Review and Adjustment:**  
   Evaluate and update salary packages to ensure fairness and competitiveness, particularly for roles and tenures with high attrition.

3. **Strengthen Onboarding and Early Support:**  
   Invest in onboarding programs and mentorship for new employees to improve early retention.

4. **Role Analysis and Enrichment:**  
   Re-examine high-attrition roles for improvements in workload, recognition, and development opportunities.

5. **Continuous Monitoring and Responsive Action:**  
   Use ongoing data analysis to adapt HR policies proactively, refining retention strategies as new insights emerge.

By implementing these strategies, HR can reduce attrition, improve employee satisfaction, and foster long-term organizational success.
